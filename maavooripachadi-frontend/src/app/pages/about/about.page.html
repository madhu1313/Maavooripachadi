Maavoori Pachadi

Heritage Andhra flavours, hand-crafted for today

We started Maavoori Pachadi to bring the taste of amma’s kitchen back to every Telugu home. From sun-cured mango avakai to slow-cooked gongura mutton, every jar carries the warmth of our village traditions and the care of our home chefs.

Grandma’s recipes, perfected over decades

Our pickles are stone-ground, sun-cured and slow-cooked exactly the way our ammamma taught us. We sift and clean every spice by hand, pound them in traditional rolu-batta and ferment in earthen jars to lock in flavour.

Each batch is limited, allowing us to taste every jar, verify spice balance and ensure it hits your table with the same aroma that fills our kitchen in Machilipatnam.

Sourcing mindful ingredients from local farms

We partner with small growers across coastal Andhra and Telangana. Our mangoes are plucked at dawn and transported in cold vans, our gingelly oil is cold pressed in Rajahmundry, and our red chilli powder is made from Guntur sannam chillies.

The result? A clean label pantry—free from preservatives, artificial colours or shortcuts. Just sun, spice, salt and the stories we grew up with.

Crafted by a women-led kitchen collective

Our kitchen in Hyderabad is run by a team of women home-chefs who have honed their pickling craft over decades. We employ hygienic stainless-steel prep stations, filtered water and daily sanitation routines, yet keep the process gently manual.
